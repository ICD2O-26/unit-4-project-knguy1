Self-Driving Cars

What are self-driving cars?

Self-driving cars are vehicles that can operate without a human driver.

What problem do they solve?

Most car accidents are caused by human error, such as distraction or speeding. Self-driving cars aim to reduce these mistakes and make roads safer. They can also help people who are unable to drive, such as elderly or disabled individuals.

How do they work?

Self-driving cars collect information using cameras, radar, and LiDAR sensors. This data is processed by AI systems that recognize objects like cars, pedestrians, and traffic lights. The car then makes decisions, such as when to stop, turn, or change lanes.

Steps the car follows

  1. Collect data from sensors
  2. Analyze the surroundings
  3. Make decisions
  4. Control the vehicle

Where are they used?

Self-driving cars are being tested and used in the transportation industry. Some companies are developing self-driving taxis and delivery vehicles. They may also be used in the future for public transportation.

Impacts

Positive Impacts

  • Reduce accidents and save lives
  • Improve convenience and accessibility
  • More efficient driving can reduce traffic

Negative Impacts

  • Job loss for drivers
  • Technology errors
  • Ethical concerns

Environmental Impact

Self-driving cars may reduce fuel use by driving more efficiently. However, more usage could increase traffic and pollution.
